Rasam salad

Home

Description

Rasam is a comforting South Indian soup known for its bold, tangy, and spicy flavors. Made with tamarind, tomatoes, and a fragrant blend of spices like black pepper, cumin, and mustard seeds, rasam is both soothing and invigorating. It’s often enjoyed with steamed rice or sipped on its own as a light, warming soup. Whether served as part of a traditional South Indian meal or as a simple home remedy for colds, rasam is the perfect balance of flavor and comfort.

Ingredients

  • 1 small lemon-sized tamarind ball
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1‐2 tsp rasam powder (or a mix of cumin, coriander, and black pepper)
  • 1‐2 green chilies, slit
  • 2‐3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 cup cooked toor dal (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • ½ tsp mustard seeds
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 1‐2 dried red chilies
  • 5‐6 curry leaves
  • Fresh coriander, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Soak tamarind in warm water and extract the juice.
  2. Mix dry ingredients in one bowl and wet ingredients in another.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients until smooth.
  4. Pour batter into cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full.
  5. Bake for the recommended time, then cool on a wire rack.
  6. Frost and decorate as desired.
